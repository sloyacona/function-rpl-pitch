Factor V Leiden heterozygosity is clinically significant for recurrent pregnancy loss. This mutation increases blood clotting risk by 3-8x and is associated with increased risk of miscarriage, particularly in the 8-10 week window when placental circulation is establishing. Your low-normal Protein S may compound this risk.

Recommended action: Discuss prophylactic anticoagulation (such as Lovenox/enoxaparin) with a maternal-fetal medicine specialist or hematologist before your next pregnancy. Many women with Factor V Leiden and RPL have successful pregnancies with anticoagulation therapy.
